The QEII Wildlands Provincial Park is an unmaintained park, which means it is free to explore! The Ganaraska Trail, however, does pass through the park and there are several access points. Located nearly 90 kilometers north of Lindsay, QEII Wildlands Provincial Park (QEIIWPP) is a spectacular area to explore on foot or by canoe. Due to its primitive and unstaffed nature, this provincial park is quiet comparted its larger nearby cousin, Algonquin Park.

The park is characteristic of this area, a checkerboard of rock barren, wetlands and lakes. The Ganaraska Trail traverses a significant portion of the northern section of this park and is one of the best backcountry hiking trails available to Ontarians. Popular day use areas exist as well, with Victoria Falls at the eastern entrance and Devil Lake at the eastern trailhead. While exploring the eastern section of the park, you can also hike the Fire Tower Loop (former location of the Lutterworth Fire Tower).


The Ganaraska Trail Marked by white blazes, this trail traverses 500 kilometers of Canadian Wildernerss. There are many local clubs which lead guided hikes for many sections of trail. Aboout 100 kilometers of trail pass through QEII Wildlands PP and can be accessed from 3 locations: Victoria Falls, Devil Lake and Near Black Creek.
