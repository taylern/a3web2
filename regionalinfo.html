About Kawartha Lakes, Ontario, Canada

The City of Kawartha Lakes is an amalgamated municipality northwest of Peterborough, Ontario. It is located along the Trent-Severn Waterway and noted for its sprawling system of two hundred and fifty lakes and rivers. Kawartha Lakes is a vibrant region with a myriad of outdoor settings and recreational opportunities.

Kawartha Lakes consists of the many smaller communities that comprise the former Victoria County, the largest of which is the town of Lindsay, Ontario. Kawartha Lakes has a permanent population of 75,000, but over 30,000 people also stay in the region on a seasonal basis. Additionally, Kawartha Lakes sees almost 1.4 million visitors each year, drawn to the area by its lakes, green spaces, and local culture.

With over 250 lakes and rivers and a number of provincial parks, The Kawartha Lakes is a vibrant region for its myriad of outdoor settings and recreational opportunities. Located just over an hour's drive north-east of the GTA, the region is easily accessible whether visiting for the day, living here seasonally, or relocating permanently.

Land Acknowledgement

Kawartha Lakes is located on the traditional lands of the Anishinaabe people.
